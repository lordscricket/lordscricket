A specially eventful cricket match spanning generations, seasons; pagelength & title-length. Led by Brother vs Brother to enable Sis v Sis and Husband v Wife.

Lording, Participle, from Lord (Intransitive verb): To insist upon or boast about so as to act in a superior manner; To act or maintain a position in a domineering manner; To rule over. To become a stat addict; and to put individual stats above your team's goal.

None of these definitions describe this moment: One week, an Aussie dreams about cricket in the day, quickly remembering pads and helmets, disregards that nonsense and dreams of tennis balls pinging off bats in the night. He awakes with new **features & stats** for Lords Cricket on his mind. By the next week he's calling 'howzat' when he reaches a hand to catch a dish at the sink. By the 3rd he's brainstorming new ways of displaying stats: for banter with his workmate (despite no more cricket). By the time the month is up he's throwing pies across the living room of his unit -- at his wife who's begrudgingly holding a bat. The final stage - he's hosting his own game of Lords Cricket well into the autumn and forcing his friends and family along. Lording - participle: To be subjected to the addiction that is Lords Cricket.

Weather: after 10 days of hitting "refresh" on forecast; and the forecast of rain facing it's own reality; Lords Cricket would go on. ANZAC day and a public holiday falls perfectly to start a weekend, I, Joshua invited along my mates, cousins, wife, parents and even grandparents as spectators for a bonus round to top off my debut season after a solitary Run-Out as a sub just wasn't enough to scratch my cricket itch. On this very grey day, a closed registration became increasingly open and players kept appearing heading down the path to the Billabong Oval. Surely all these people weren't here to play cricket on a grey autumn's day? Six debutantes and a guest player; this game had it all, including generations that truly spanned 1948 (Donald Bradman's historical English tour): Barry Rimmer through 2000 (Waugh, captaining the start of 16 win-streak): his grandson J Miller (yours truly) & two grand-daughters, H & S Rimmer.

Hosting my first game; I was a bit distracted and someone told me I was opening the batting for Waugh2000. An opening stand of 68 with yours truly, Joshua Miller [30 (35)] and Sam Tolhurst's efficient 27 (19) with his first career 6s looked solid until J.Miller fell, and debutante Sascha Moseley (one of two that doesn't require a first initial) got out in classic Lord's fashion, skying his second ball off his top edge to keeper Andrew I. Turner who without anyone nearby to confuse, took the catch with ease. 1/68 became 2/69 became 3/75 in a flash. 4/75 how did we get here, 5/75... a... hat-trick? And just like that with her first five bowls on the pitch had taken four wickets immediately leaping the young H Rimmer into Lords Cricket fame finishing 4/16 and in Current Rating is at 412 leapfrogging two of her wickets; Tolhurst (#18) and Stephen Lang (#11) to fly into #8 of the Bowling Dream Team with the Strike Rate (raw) of 8. The quality of the wickets? Well this is Lords cricket. Only the most exceptional and unreproducible moments are described. Thus #2: S. Lang's bails fell; and that's most of what we know. Hit-Wicket is a brutal way to go, particularly when you're not entirely sure how your castle fell.
